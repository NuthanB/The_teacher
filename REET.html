REET (Rajasthan Eligibility Examination for Teachers) Exam Guide

Introduction to REET Exam:

The Rajasthan Eligibility Examination for Teachers (REET) is conducted by the Board of Secondary Education Rajasthan (BSER) to determine the eligibility of candidates for teaching positions in schools across Rajasthan.

It is a mandatory exam for those aspiring to become teachers in Rajasthan state government schools.

Eligibility Criteria:

Eligibility criteria for REET exam:

  • Candidates must have a minimum educational qualification as specified by the BSER.
  • There may be different eligibility criteria for primary and upper primary level teaching positions.
  • Candidates must fulfill age criteria as per government regulations.
  • Relaxation in age and educational qualifications may be provided to certain categories as per government norms.

Exam Pattern:

Level Subjects Number of Questions Total Marks Duration
Level 1 (Primary Teacher for Class 1 to 5) Child Development and Pedagogy, Language I, Language II, Mathematics, Environmental Studies 150 150 2.5 hours
Level 2 (Upper Primary Teacher for Class 6 to 8) Child Development and Pedagogy, Language I, Language II, Mathematics & Science, Social Studies/Social Science 150 150 2.5 hours

Syllabus:

The syllabus for REET exam includes:

  • Child Development and Pedagogy
  • Languages (Hindi, English, Sanskrit, Urdu)
  • Mathematics
  • Environmental Studies (for Level 1)
  • Science (for Level 2)
  • Social Studies/Social Science (for Level 2)

Preparation Tips:

  • Understand the exam pattern and syllabus thoroughly.
  • Practice previous years' question papers and mock tests.
  • Focus on strengthening subject-specific knowledge for the level you're applying for.
  • Refer to standard textbooks and study materials recommended by experts.
  • Stay updated with current affairs and educational trends.
  • Join coaching classes or online study groups for guidance and support.
  • Manage your time effectively during the exam and practice time-bound mock tests.
