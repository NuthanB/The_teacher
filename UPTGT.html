UP TGT/PGT Exam Guide

UP Trained Graduate Teacher (TGT) Exam:

The UP Trained Graduate Teacher (TGT) Exam is conducted by the Uttar Pradesh Secondary Education Service Selection Board (UPSESSB) to select eligible candidates for teaching positions in secondary schools.

It is a state-level examination for candidates aspiring to become TGTs in various subjects.

Eligibility Criteria for UP TGT:

  • Candidates must have a Bachelor's degree in Education (B.Ed.) or equivalent from a recognized university.
  • They should have completed or are in the process of completing the Teacher Training Programme recognized by the National Council for Teacher Education (NCTE).
  • Additional eligibility criteria may vary depending on the subject and school board requirements.

Exam Pattern for UP TGT:

Subject Number of Questions Total Marks Duration
General Knowledge 20 20 2 hours
Subject-specific Questions 100 100 2 hours

UP Post Graduate Teacher (PGT) Exam:

The UP Post Graduate Teacher (PGT) Exam is conducted by the Uttar Pradesh Secondary Education Service Selection Board (UPSESSB) to select eligible candidates for teaching positions in higher secondary schools.

It is a state-level examination for candidates aspiring to become PGTs in various subjects.

Eligibility Criteria for UP PGT:

  • Candidates must have a Master's degree in the relevant subject from a recognized university.
  • They should have completed or are in the process of completing the Teacher Training Programme recognized by the National Council for Teacher Education (NCTE).
  • Additional eligibility criteria may vary depending on the subject and school board requirements.

Exam Pattern for UP PGT:

Subject Number of Questions Total Marks Duration
General Knowledge 20 20 2 hours
Subject-specific Questions 150 150 2 hours

Preparation Tips:

  • Understand the exam pattern and syllabus for the specific subject you are applying for.
  • Practice previous years' question papers and mock tests to improve time management and familiarity with the exam format.
  • Focus on strengthening subject-specific knowledge and pedagogical skills.
  • Refer to standard textbooks and study materials recommended by experts.
  • Stay updated with current affairs and educational trends.
  • Join coaching classes or online study groups for guidance and support.
